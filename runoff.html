Runoff Analysis

1 Model Building

The team used the stormwater management model EPA SWMM 5.1 to perform a runoff volume simulation for the site area. The system runoff from the entire site area consisting of pipes, drains, and storage facilities can be calculated using site area data.

a) Rainfall Simulation

The EPA SWMM uses rain gauges to represent rainfall input to the system. The team assumes extreme rainfall scenario and creates a rainfall intensity of 0.50 inches/hour (12.7 cm/hour) over a 24-hour storm time series to simulate rainfall at the site, as shown in Figure 7-1.

Figure 7-1 Time series rainfall

b) Wind speed simulation

The team calculated the average value of monthly average wind speed for two years by counting the monthly average wind speed in Nanjing in 2019 and 2020, and assumed it as the characteristic wind speed of the site area for simulation in the EPA SWMM modeling process. The wind speed statistics are shown in the Table 7-1 below.

c) Drainage System Simulation

The EPA SWMM simulates the surface runoff by dividing it into sub-catchments transported through the established pipe conveyance network to a storage facility and finally to an outfall. The team generalized the site area's drainage system into 32 sub-catchments, 40 stormwater pipe sections, 39 stormwaters well nodes, 2 water storage facilities, and 2 end-of-pipe outfalls, as shown in Figure 7-2.

Figure 7-2 The model of the drainage system

d) Infiltration Simulation

The team used the Horton infiltration model in the EPA SWMM to simulate stormwater infiltration into the sub-catchments unsaturated upper soil zone. Since the site area consists of USDA Group A soils, we selected a maximum infiltration rate of 12 mm/hr and a minimum infiltration rate of 0.06 mm/hr. for the Horton infiltration model parameters according to the U.S. Soil Classification System

3.Summary

The team used the stormwater management model EPA SWMM 5.1 as the primary research tool to simulate and calculate the site area's drainage index parameters for the pipe drains, sub catchment, water storage facilities, and LID settings in the runoff system by establishing the runoff system of the site area. The calculated index parameters are all in line with the Outdoor Drainage Design Code GB50014-2006 and the requirements of the competition, so the drainage design pipe network in the site area can basically meet the drainage requirements of the site area, the road surface will not produce ponding, and the rainwater runoff from the storm is contained in the boundary site.
